ARCOMAGE rules

Acromage is deceptively simple. You start out with five cards and a set amount of resources and resource producers (sources), along with a tower and wall. The goal is to fulfill one of three conditions: To reduce your opponent's tower to zero or build your tower to the limit 100. Conversely, you lose if your tower is reduced to zero or you let your opponent's tower get too high.
The turn order is simple: first you draw up to five cards (your first turn), collect resources equal to how many producers (sources) you have (If you have five mines for example, you get five bricks this turn) and then choose a card to play (left-click on card area) or discard (left-click on discard area). You can only play or discard one card on your turn (unless you play a card that allows you to play again), of which you will again draw back up to five. At any one time, you'll have a hand of five cards.
There are three types of cards: Orange cards use bricks, which are produced by mines. They generally work on your wall. Blue cards use gems, which are produced by magic. They generally concern the construction of your tower. Green cards use beasts, which are produced by dungeons. They generally work as your offense. There are exceptions of course, but this is the general rule.
When attacking your enemy (using a card that inflicts damage), any damage will be dealt to their wall first. If any damage is left over, the remainder will be inflicted onto their tower. Thus the wall works as a protective shield for your tower. Of course, there are ways around it, with cards (usually blue) that inflict direct damage on a tower.
